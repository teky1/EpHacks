About

Welcome to the Epsilon Hackathon, hosted by FTC Team #20124! We are a 2nd-year robotics team competing in the FIRST Tech Challenge comprised of 13 students from Centennial High School. Our vision is to help our local community by supporting local businesses, performing outreach, and spreading awareness of FTC and STEM through events and workshops.


This hackathon will feature 15 algorithm-based questions and various challenges relating to programming. This year, the Epsilon Hackathon will take place at the Miller Branch Library in the Avalon Room (9421 Frederick Rd, Ellicott City, MD 21042). Food will be served, and participants have the opportunity to win prizes!

FAQ

When is EpHacks? EpHacks will take place on Wednesday, January 4th 2022 at the Miller Branch Library
Who can attend EpHacks? EpHacks is open to all middle and highschool students
Is there food? Yes dinner and snacks will be provided. If you have a dietary restriction please indicate that in the registration form.
What do I need to bring? To participate in EpHacks all you need is a laptop (or school Chromebook) and your charging cord.
What programming languages should I know? You can use basically any langauge for challenges, but we recommend participants use either Python, Java, or C++ for the competition problems.
How much programming experience should I have? EpHacks is targetted towards beginner and intermediate programmers, so you should come even if you can only barely write a Hello World program!
