About

Welcome to the EpHacks, hosted by FTC Team #20124! This hackathon will feature a series of algorithm-based questions and various challenges relating to programming. This year, EpHacks will take place at the Miller Branch Library in the Patapsco Room (9421 Frederick Rd, Ellicott City, MD 21042). Food will be served, and participants have the opportunity to win prizes! If you have any questions, please contact us at centennialroboticsinc@gmail.com.

FAQ

When/Where is EpHacks? EpHacks will take place on Wednesday, January 4th 2022 at the Miller Branch Library in the Patapsco Room (9421 Frederick Rd, Ellicott City, MD 21042)
Is there food? Yes dinner and snacks will be provided. If you have a dietary restriction please indicate that in the registration form.
What is the format of the competition? The competition will consist of algorithm-based problems and open-ended challenges. The challenges are attainable for programmers of all experience levels. The problems, however, are more hardcore
What do I need to bring? To participate in EpHacks all you need is a laptop (or Chromebook) and your charging cord.
What programming languages should I know? You can use basically any langauge for challenges, but we recommend participants use either Python, Java, or C++ for the competition problems.
How do prizes work? Prizes will be awarded to top scorers in the competition, and there will also be prizes raffled out to everyone so that everyone has the chance to take something home!
How much programming experience should I have? EpHacks is targetted towards beginner and intermediate programmers, so you should come even if you can only barely write a Hello World program!
Who can attend EpHacks? EpHacks is open to all middle and highschool students
How do teams work? You will get a chance to choose your team of up to three people at the beginning of the competition. We recommend bringing friends to team up with!

Team Epsilon #20124

We are Team Epsilon #20124, a 2nd-year robotics team competing in the FIRST Tech Challenge comprised of students from Centennial High School. Our vision is to help our local community by supporting local businesses, performing outreach, and spreading awareness of FTC and STEM through events and workshops.

If you have any questions or concerns, please contact us at centennialroboticsinc@gmail.com.
